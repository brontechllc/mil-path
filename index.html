The real problem
200,000 service members
separate every year.
And most of them get handed a pamphlet and a 3-day TAP class. Here's what that actually looks like.

Resumes that don't translate

"Managed logistics operations for 200-person element" means nothing to a civilian hiring manager. Your experience is real — it's just not being communicated right.

No real roadmap

TAP class gives you a checklist. It doesn't give you a plan tailored to your Military Occupational Code, your goals, your timeline, or your family situation. Everyone gets the same generic playbook.

Benefits left on the table

GI Bill, disability ratings, VA home loans, state-specific programs — most separating members don't know what they qualify for or how to stack benefits correctly.

No one who's actually been there

The people running transition assistance programs are often contractors reading slides. They've never worn the uniform. They don't know what you've actually done.
